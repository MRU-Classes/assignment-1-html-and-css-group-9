About Us

Who: Newcomer children and youth, ages 8 to 18, are the primary participants in this program.

What: The program features a dynamic mix of indoor activities and outdoor, sports-based programming designed to promote health, belonging, and community connection. Offerings include field trips, community exploration, creative arts, educational workshops, and organized sports.

Where: Based in Calgary, Alberta, the program operates through community centers, parks, recreational facilities, and cultural or immigrant-serving organizations.

When: Full-day, structured programming runs weekly from late June through late August.

Why: Our mission is to help newcomer children and youth feel seen, supported, and valued. By providing consistent opportunities for connection, growth, and recreation, Pathways to Belonging empowers participants to thrive in their new environment and helps build a more inclusive and connected community.

Our Services

  • Youth summer programs (ages 8–18)
  • Cultural integration workshops
  • Support for newcomers
  • Language support activities

Weekly Structure Overview

Each week features a 7-hour session focused on a different theme. Over 13 weeks, participants engage in diverse activities that align with the program's goals: health, integration, and community connection. This structure encourages relationship-building, skill development, and a welcoming environment.

Weekly scheduling Monday-Friday for 5 weeks

Week Time Activity Details
Week 1 10:00am – 5:00pm Introduction & Icebreaker Activities
  • Team-building games and goal setting
  • Create and decorate notebooks and name tags
  • Guided painting session
  • Light outdoor games (e.g., frisbee, relay races)
Week 2 10:00am – 5:00pm Arts & Crafts Extravaganza
  • Painting or pottery with a local artist
  • Creative crafts: jewelry, rock painting, tie-dye
  • Organized sports and outdoor games
  • Group picnic
Week 3 Field Trip: Glenbow Ranch (All-Day Event)
Week 4 10:00am – 5:00pm Crafting Week
  • Pottery or painting with a local artist
  • Clay modeling and sculpture activities
  • Outdoor sports and games
Week 5 10:00am – 5:00pm Guest Speaker: Indigenous Storytelling & Art
  • Storytelling session on Indigenous history and culture
  • Art project: beadwork, dreamcatchers, cultural symbols
  • Traditional Indigenous games
